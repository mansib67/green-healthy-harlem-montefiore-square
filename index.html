Project overview

Project title: Green and Healthy Harlem: Redesigning Montefiore Square for shade, comfort, planting, and public well-being.

Project goal: improve the plaza as a healthier public space with stronger tree canopy, better daily seating choice, cooler walking paths, and a clearer wellness identity.

Essential question: How can Montefiore Square become greener, more comfortable, and more inviting to stay in without losing the structure people already recognize?

The design does not treat the site as empty. It treats the site as already improved but still incomplete in comfort terms. The strongest opportunity is to push shade, softer planting layers, and healthier everyday use much further.

Problem definition and site context

Montefiore Square already has lawn, paving, storefront edges, benches, and young trees. The problem is not that the site lacks structure. The problem is that the plaza still reads more as a route through space than a place designed for long comfort.

  • Shade: main walking lines and sitting zones still depend on trees that have not matured enough.
  • Seating choice: fixed benches do not offer enough variety for privacy, group size, or preferred sun and shade.
  • Planting depth: greener edges are needed at eye level, not only as lawn at ground level.
  • Wellness identity: the plaza needs clearer cues for walking, pausing, resting, and evening use.

The redesign therefore focuses on comfort, cooling, greenery, and healthier daily use rather than on erasing the site and starting from zero.

Comfort and climate analysis

The brief for Green and Healthy Harlem asks for a comfort and climate analysis plus a shade and seating study. The main findings are simple: more canopy is needed over the most used paths, planting should work harder as climate infrastructure, and sitting should feel flexible instead of fixed.

Midday shade

Primary routes and the central paved zones still feel exposed before the canopy fully matures.

Seating choice

Most sitting is fixed, so users cannot easily choose stronger shade, social spacing, or privacy.

Planting needs

Bioswale edges, understory planting, and seasonal meadow layers can cool and soften the public room.

Healthy use

A plaza that supports walking loops, pause space, quiet retreat, and evening comfort can invite longer stays.

Final redesign proposal

The selected Green Wellness Plaza concept keeps the plaza recognizable but upgrades it into a greener, healthier, and more comfortable public landscape. It focuses on stronger shade over the most used paths, planted edges that cool and organize the space, and furniture that supports both movement and staying.

  • fuller tree canopy over walking paths and seating zones
  • bioswales, understory planting, and seasonal flowering edges
  • moveable chairs and cafe tables for flexible daily use
  • a simple wellness loop for short walks and casual exercise
  • warmer lighting for calmer evening atmosphere

The goal is not to overprogram the site. The goal is to make it easier to walk, sit, cool down, pause, and come back.

Implementation timeline

  1. Phase 1: add moveable furniture, temporary planting, and walking-loop markers.
  2. Phase 2: plant additional trees and install bioswales and understory edges.
  3. Phase 3: complete lighting upgrades and establish long-term maintenance routines.

Maintenance note

The healthiest version of the plaza depends on regular watering during plant establishment, seasonal pruning and replanting, litter control around seating clusters, and a basic operations plan for moveable furniture.

AI workflow and human judgment

The handbook says students should begin with real site views, use those views as visual references, generate multiple options, compare them, and select the strongest versions. That is the workflow used here.

Step 1

Collect real Google Maps screenshots from multiple Montefiore Square angles.

Step 2

Generate redesign studies for aerial structure, shaded corridors, meditation zones, market variation, night comfort, and active healthy use.

Step 3

Compare the real views and AI outputs, then reject images that drift too far from the original camera angle or the Group 5 theme.

Step 4

Keep the images that best support shade, comfort, planting, and public well-being.

AI helped with exploration, but the final ordering, concept selection, captions, and project framing stayed human.
